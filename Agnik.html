Agnik

Introduction

Fires except for those started by natural agents are an environmental pollutant phenomenon, with exclusive, direct dependence on social behavior. It is no secret that local fire is one of the major causes of loss of human life and if not checked right now it will perhaps go on to annihilate mankind. Many areas like tall buildings, houses on narrow roads often become inaccessible for firefighters. Sometimes fire expands rapidly which can destroy a huge area in a matter of minutes. This generates a need for products such as a proactive aid to control local fires. To overcome this problem, many products like fireball extinguisher, fire extinguisher pump have been made. Existing products on fire extinguishers are mainly non-reusable and expensive. Agnik has been proposed as a reusable and user-friendly product at a cheaper rate than existing products so that it can cater to larger population segments. Agnik, dry powder based proactive and reusable fire safety equipment which can fit in the right spaces and ensures you a household safe from fire hazards. Two use cases of the product has been worked out extensively.

HYPEREX

Fuse-Box Problem

When it comes to household electrics, people's safety is paramount. Flickering lights, circuit tripping, and damaged appliances can all be a sign of electrical problems on your home circuit. Any of these problems can result in unexpected fires spreading and engulfing sometimes the entire building. Thus, a proactive way to deal with these fires at the core itself is much needed. HyperEx is such a device that can help douse fires before they become too big to handle.

Solution: Hyper Extinguisher

The HyperEx is a hyperboloidal structure, from where it derives its name - Hyperboloidal Extinguisher. The design is influenced by natural cooling towers which provide very efficient and cost-effective airflow as well as additional stability. In addition to this, the hyperboloidal shape also ensures the uniform dispersion of dry powder in all directions. The upper end of the hyperboloid is capped with a torispherical wire mesh. Next to the mesh are slots for placing the battery and a compartment of melamine phosphate powder. These slots are made detachable in order to refill melamine phosphate or to change the battery as and when required. Small hole openings are made at the base of the hyperboloid structure and it is through these holes that dry powder is blown out serving as an extinguishing agent for the fire. At the side of the hyperboloidal structure, a temperature sensor and battery are placed.

Working Principle

As the sensors detect a temperature peak beyond the threshold, a series of actions is triggered. The DC motor starts rotating thus bringing the impeller in motion. Dry powder drops from the MP. As the impeller starts rotating and sucking air from the hind mesh, the dry powder gets circulated with the flowing air and is pushed through the small holes at the base of the hyperboloid. As the powder spreads in the fuse box dousing the fire and the temperature of the fuse box drops, the sensors detect the change and reverse the shaft action thus closing the MP compartment and cutting off the power supply to the DC motor. The motor is ready to be used for the next incident of temperature rise. Once the powder runs out, the user can conveniently change the MP phosphate compartment which would be sold in the market.

GUNEX

Travelling Fireman Problem

When it comes to extinguishing a fire in narrow roads or in tall buildings, it becomes very difficult for firefighters to perform rescue operations in remote sections of these cramped buildings esp. when their limited length water hoses and heavy CO2 cylinders are unreachable. Moreover, the amount of time spent by each person extinguishing the fire is limited by the amount of extinguisher material available in a situation when every minute spent climbing up a tall building result in precious. Many fire hostages die every year due to suffocation when trapped in the fire. However, these problems can be handled easily by compact devices that can be carried around in a firefighter's vest thus giving convenience over a water hose and heavy cylinders. Moreover, if the device is reusable it can also be cost saving over one-time-use CO2 cylinders.

Solution: Gun Extinguisher

GunEx is a handheld firefighting device designed for easing the rescue operations of firefighters in remote corners of buildings. It is a compact rechargeable device of the size of a hairdryer that can comfortably fit in their vests. GunEx is a design motivation from HyperEx itself, thus utilizing concepts of biomimicry for efficient design and optimal performance. It is void of any sensors rather than uses a manual trigger which can be used by the firefighter as an when required. A detachable melamine phosphate compartment is present towards the nozzle, which can be refilled or changed whenever the powder runs out.

Working Principle

The triggering mechanism utilizes a lily impeller and a rotary valve. As soon as the trigger is pulled the lily impeller is rotated via a DC motor which sucks in the outside air from the rear end of the handheld instrument and directs it towards the rotary valve located in front. The pulling of the trigger also causes a mechanical pull on the case which makes the MP powder drop on the rotary valve through a fine mesh. The agitated air makes the valve rotate about its axis. This causes the Melamine Phosphate to mix with the high-velocity air stream before it exits the instrument and reaches the target location. As the trigger is released the case recloses the valve opening and the DC motor stops rotating.
