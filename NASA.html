Introduction

In order to facilitate modifications from an Industrial Design perspective, it is important that the critical to quality parameters are addressed in the defining of performance measurement for any product. In case of an adequate Excretion Management System, all essential concepts, such as Functioning Capability, Ergonomic and Aesthetic alignment, Ease of Use and Objective Addressal to the stated problem statement, need to be defined and measured accordingly.

In order to revamp the current design in order to adapt to the necessary use case environment, it is important to address the entire topological design definition from the operational perception of MicroGravity and Lunar Gravity Use cases. From multiple references, it is observational that a predefined protocol has to be addressed to achieve successful discharge and system dispatch, and accordingly in the proposed solution design these objectives have been attempted to be resolved.

Design Overview

For adequate design modifications, some concepts from GD&T Design analysis, DFMA, DFR and similar principles are adopted to revamp the Excretion Management System. It is understood that an ergonomic, portable and capable system requirement is present that could satisfy the necessities of both male and female astronauts and could be operational for minimal efforts. In the proposed solution system, a more topological approach-oriented design definition is suggested for the commode cover along with an independent faecal motion regulatory system. The use case is concerned for both the user genders and to support independent and simultaneous urination and defecation along with a proper disposal design solution. The entire solution system should be compact and portable and also adaptable to the environment variations.

Thus, in our solution case, the commode cover is reshaped to a more ergonomic design that ensures: ‚óè Astronauts can properly orient themselves for defecation arrangement through a more physiological approach by introducing silicon polymer marking pads for orientation assessment topologically. ‚óè Urination caps and modules can be introduced in front of the faecal pot on an inlet hose so that the user can urinate simultaneously during defecation. An additional outlet from the front coupling is to be independently used if only urination is required, so that use case retainability is preserved. ‚óè Menstrual cups or modules are inserted on the same faecal pot hose for urination, and the flow cab can be rerouted back into the commode pot for discharge. ‚óè Emergency Vomit coupling is available to the astronauts for all the time. Since the patch is closed, there will be no odour present & the vomiting can be done fast through the plug cover attached on top.
